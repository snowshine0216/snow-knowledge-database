# Best ML Courses 2026 (Marina Wyss's Top-5 Ranking)

Marina Wyss reviewed **50+ broad-coverage, financially accessible ML courses** and ranked the top 5 across four axes — comprehensiveness, interactivity, price, ratings — each scored 0-2.5 for a /10 total. The headline finding subverts the standard internet answer: **Andrew Ng's celebrated Coursera course comes in last** (8.0) because it stops at model training and ignores production, covering only ~30% of what an ML engineer actually does. The winner is the relatively obscure, free **ML Zoomcamp by DataTalks.Club** (8.8) — the only course that covers both algorithms and full deployment. The meta-lesson: **no single course is enough — stack one strong on algorithms with one strong on MLOps.**

## Key Concepts

- **Wyss's 4-axis rubric** (each 0-2.5, /10 total): comprehensiveness (algorithms *and* production?), interactivity (hands-on coding vs passive lectures), price, ratings/sentiment. Use it before buying any course — popularity ≠ fit.
- **Comprehensiveness gap**: every course on the list maxes 2.2/2.5 on this axis. No single course covers both algorithm depth and production end-to-end with full quality.
- **Stack pattern**: pair an algorithms-strong course (Andrew Ng or DataCamp ML Scientist) with an MLOps-strong one (Made with ML or DataCamp ML Engineer). DataCamp's $35/mo bundles both tracks under one subscription.

## The Top 5

| # | Course | Score | Cost | Sweet spot |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Andrew Ng — Coursera ML Specialization** | 8.0 | $49/mo | Math intuition for *why* algorithms work; zero MLOps; ~30% of ML-engineer skills |
| 4 | **Made with ML** (Goku Mahandas) | 8.1 | Free (45k★ on GitHub) | Best free MLOps walkthrough; algo side is reference material, not curriculum |
| 3 | **DataCamp ML Scientist Track** | 8.6 | $35/mo | 26 courses / 85 hrs; broad algo coverage with constant hands-on coding; no production |
| 2 | **DataCamp ML Engineer Track** | 8.7 | $35/mo (same sub) | 14 courses / 44 hrs; almost entirely production — Docker, MLflow, ETL/ELT, CI/CD |
| 1 | **ML Zoomcamp** (DataTalks.Club) | 8.8 | Free | 4 months / 160 hrs; only course covering both sides — FastAPI, Docker, AWS Lambda, Kubernetes; high barrier (no IDE), 10k+ student Slack |

## Key Takeaways

- **Stack two courses, don't pick one.** The best single score is 8.8/10; comprehensiveness caps at 2.2/2.5 anywhere. Pair an algorithms course with an MLOps one for full coverage.
- **Production coverage is the missing axis in classic courses.** Andrew Ng "teaches you to train models beautifully — and then stops." Hiring increasingly tests Docker / CI/CD / monitoring skills the canonical course skips entirely.
- **Free only beats paid when you're genuinely self-directed.** Made with ML and ML Zoomcamp are excellent but demand high self-direction (no exercises, no IDE, no graded homework). Paid platforms (Coursera, DataCamp) buy structure and feedback.
- **Hands-on typing still compounds in the AI-coding era.** Wyss explicitly defends manual code-typing over passive video — same "discomfort is the learning" thesis from her [[learning-retention-system]] companion video.
- **Use the rubric, not the brand.** Andrew Ng has 4.8M learners and 4.9★ but ranks #5 because two axes cap his score. Score courses yourself before buying.
