# Chapter 5: Why Euler's Number \(e\) Is Special

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/eulers-number

Essence:
- Exponential functions are characterized by being proportional to their own slope.
- Among \(a^x\), exactly one base has derivative equal to itself: \(e\).
- \(e\) is the natural growth base and appears when continuous compounding is pushed to the limit.

Core equations:
$$
\frac{d}{dx}a^x = (\ln a)\,a^x
$$

$$
\frac{d}{dx}e^x = e^x
$$

$$
e=\lim_{n\to\infty}\left(1+\frac{1}{n}\right)^n
$$
