# Chapter 2: The Paradox of the Derivative

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/derivatives

Essence:
- Instantaneous rate sounds paradoxical: at one instant there is no "change over time."
- The resolution is the limit process: average rate over a shrinking interval approaches a stable value.
- The derivative is the local linear behavior of a function.

Core equations:
$$
f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}
$$

$$
v(t)=\frac{ds}{dt}
$$

$$
\Delta s \approx f'(x)\,\Delta x \quad (\text{for small } \Delta x)
$$
