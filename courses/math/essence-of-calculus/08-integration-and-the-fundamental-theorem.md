# Chapter 8: Integration and the Fundamental Theorem

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/integration

Essence:
- Integration accumulates tiny quantities into a total.
- A definite integral is the limit of a sum of thin rectangles/strips.
- The Fundamental Theorem links accumulation and rates of change, making integration and differentiation inverse operations.

Core equations:
$$
\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = \lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{k=1}^n f(x_k^*)\,\Delta x
$$

$$
F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\,dt \Rightarrow F'(x)=f(x)
$$

$$
\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b)-F(a)
$$
