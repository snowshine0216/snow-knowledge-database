# Chapter 1: The Essence of Calculus

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/essence-of-calculus

Essence:
- Calculus starts from one core move: break a quantity into tiny pieces, understand each piece, then add everything back.
- The circle-area problem is the gateway idea: area can be reconstructed from thin rings.
- This viewpoint naturally introduces both integration (add tiny contributions) and differentiation (track tiny changes), and hints they are inverses.

Core equations:
$$
A(r) = \pi r^2
$$

$$
dA \approx 2\pi r\,dr
$$

$$
\int_0^R 2\pi r\,dr = \pi R^2
$$
