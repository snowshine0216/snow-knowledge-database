# Chapter 6: Implicit Differentiation

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/implicit-differentiation

Essence:
- Some curves are defined by constraints \(F(x,y)=0\), not explicit formulas \(y=f(x)\).
- Differentiate both sides while treating \(y\) as a function of \(x\).
- The chain rule explains why \(dy/dx\) appears when differentiating \(y\)-terms.

Core equations:
$$
F(x,y)=0
$$

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\,y^2 = 2y\frac{dy}{dx}
$$

$$
x^2+y^2=1 \Rightarrow 2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0 \Rightarrow \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}
$$
