# Chapter 12: Euler's Formula (via Group-Theory View)

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/eulers-formula-via-group-theory

Essence:
- Euler's formula is not a coincidence; it reflects deep structure linking exponentials, rotations, and complex numbers.
- Complex multiplication encodes rotation and scaling, and \(e^{i\theta}\) traces the unit circle.
- Group-theoretic symmetry perspective explains why trig and exponential expressions are two faces of the same operation.

Core equations:
$$
e^{i\theta}=\cos\theta+i\sin\theta
$$

$$
e^{i\pi}=-1
$$

$$
e^{i(\alpha+\beta)}=e^{i\alpha}e^{i\beta}
$$
