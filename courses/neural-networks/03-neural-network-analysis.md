## Summary
- Uses an interactive trained network to inspect what the model is actually doing after optimization.
- Shows that even a small network can reach strong MNIST accuracy, while mistakes often appear on genuinely ambiguous digits.
- Highlights that hidden-layer neurons are not always cleanly interpretable as textbook edge detectors.
- Encourages empirical probing: perturbing inputs and watching activations helps build intuition about internal representations.
- Bridges intuition from architecture/training to the upcoming mechanics of gradient computation.
