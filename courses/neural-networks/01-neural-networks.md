## Summary
- Introduces a simple handwritten-digit classifier to ground the idea of neural networks in a concrete task.
- Defines a neuron as a weighted sum of previous activations plus a bias, followed by a nonlinear activation function.
- Shows why layered composition matters: lower layers detect simple patterns while later layers combine them into higher-level decisions.
- Reframes the whole network as one big function from 784 pixel values to 10 output scores, then to probabilities.
- Sets up the central learning question: how to choose weights and biases automatically from examples rather than hard-coding rules.
