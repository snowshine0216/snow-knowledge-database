## Summary
- Explores mechanistic interpretability evidence that many factual associations are stored in transformer MLP blocks.
- Decomposes the MLP computation into linear projection, biasing, nonlinearity (ReLU), and projection back.
- Uses concrete examples (like athlete-sport associations) to illustrate how neurons may encode reusable features.
- Discusses superposition, where multiple concepts can share representational directions.
- Balances insight with caution: interpretations are partial and not a complete theory of model memory.
