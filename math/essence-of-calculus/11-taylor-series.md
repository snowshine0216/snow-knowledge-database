# Chapter 11: Taylor Series

Source:
- https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/taylor-series

Essence:
- A smooth function can be approximated near a point by a polynomial built from derivatives at that point.
- Each additional term captures more local behavior and improves approximation.
- Many functions become computationally tractable through series expansion.

Core equations:
$$
f(x)=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x-a)^n
$$

$$
e^x=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{x^n}{n!}
$$

$$
\sin x=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^n\frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!},\qquad
\cos x=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^n\frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!}
$$
